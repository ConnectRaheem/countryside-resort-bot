GILGIT CITY - TRAVEL INFORMATION FOR GUESTS
=============================================

TOP PLACES TO VISIT NEAR GILGIT
---------------------------------
1. Kargah Buddha - Ancient 7th century rock carving of Buddha, 10 min from city
2. Naltar Valley - Famous for colorful lakes and skiing, 40 km from Gilgit
3. Rakaposhi Viewpoint - Stunning views of Rakaposhi (7788m) peak
4. Attabad Lake - Stunning turquoise lake in Hunza, 2 hrs from Gilgit
5. Baltit Fort - Historic fort in Karimabad Hunza, 2 hrs away
6. Khunjerab Pass - Highest paved border crossing in world (4693m), China border
7. Fairy Meadows - Base camp views of Nanga Parbat, the killer mountain
8. Gilgit River Junction - Where Gilgit and Hunza rivers meet
9. Khomarabad Gorge - Beautiful narrow gorge near Gilgit
10. Nomal Valley - Green valley with river, 30 km from Gilgit

POPULAR TREKS FROM GILGIT
--------------------------
- Rakaposhi Base Camp Trek (2-3 days)
- Haramosh Base Camp Trek (3-4 days)  
- Rush Lake Trek - highest lake in Pakistan (4694m)
- Minapin Glacier Trek
- Diran Peak Base Camp

BEST TIME TO VISIT GILGIT
--------------------------
- Spring (April-May): Blossoms, green valleys, mild weather
- Summer (June-August): Peak season, all roads open, festivals
- Autumn (September-October): Golden foliage, clear skies, fewer crowds
- Winter (November-March): Cold, some roads closed, snow on peaks

GETTING TO GILGIT
-----------------
- By Air: PIA flights from Islamabad (45 min) - weather dependent
- By Road: Karakoram Highway (KKH) from Islamabad - 18-20 hours
- Babusar Top route (via Naran): Open June 5-15 to September 5-15 only

WEATHER IN GILGIT
-----------------
- Summer (July-Aug): 25-35°C during day, cool nights
- Spring/Autumn: 15-25°C pleasant
- Winter: Can drop below 0°C, snowfall possible

LOCAL TIPS FOR GUESTS
---------------------
- Currency: Pakistani Rupees (PKR) - carry cash, ATMs available in city
- Language: Urdu and Shina widely spoken, English understood in tourist areas  
- Dress: Modest dress recommended, especially outside resort
- Altitude: Gilgit is at 1500m - some guests may feel mild altitude effects
- Mobile network: All major Pakistani networks work in Gilgit city
- Friday: Some shops close for Friday prayers midday

FOOD IN GILGIT
--------------
- Must try: Chapshoro (meat filled bread), Diram Phitti, local apricot products
- Gilgit Bazaar has local restaurants serving Pakistani and Chinese food
- Countryside Resort serves Pakistani, Local and Chinese cuisine


GILGIT CITY - NEARBY PLACES & LOCAL GUIDE
==========================================

PLACES TO VISIT NEAR GILGIT CITY
----------------------------------

1. Kargah Buddha (Yshani)
   - Ancient rock-carved Buddha statue dating back to 4th-7th century AD
   - Located in Kargah Valley, just a few km from Gilgit city
   - Nearest tourist point from the city
   - Also includes ruins of a Buddhist monastery and Hainzal Stupa
   - The people of Kargah Valley are very welcoming and friendly

2. Kargah Nala
   - Beautiful valley on the way to Kargah Buddha
   - Scenic route with stunning greenery
   - Perfect for a short day trip from the resort

3. Jutial Nala Track
   - Popular hiking track right within Gilgit city
   - Great for morning walks and short hikes
   - Beautiful views of Gilgit city and surrounding mountains

4. KIU Track (Karakoram International University Track)
   - Popular walking and hiking track near KIU campus
   - Scenic views of the mountains
   - Easy to access from the resort

5. Naltar Valley
   - Located 34 km from Gilgit city
   - Famous for stunning colourful lakes (Satrangi Lake)
   - Dense pine forests, green hills and snow-covered mountains
   - PAF Ski Resort - skiing available in winter
   - Ski championship held here every year
   - 3-4 hour trek to Naltar Lake from upper valley

6. Nomal Valley
   - Beautiful green valley with river, located 30 km from Gilgit
   - Perfect for a day trip
   - Serene and less crowded than other valleys

7. Bagrote Valley
   - Scenic valley perfect for trekking
   - Bewildering pastures and bewitching lakes
   - Towering mountain peaks

8. Danyore Valley
   - Located 10 km from Gilgit on Karakoram Highway towards Hunza
   - Famous Beranu Suspension Bridge built by locals
   - Chinese Graveyard historical site
   - Best views of south face of Rakaposhi peak

9. Gilgit Bazaar
   - Main commercial hub of Gilgit city
   - Great place to shop for local handicrafts and souvenirs
   - Busy and vibrant local market atmosphere

10. NLI Market (Northern Light Infantry Market)
    - Famous traditional market in Gilgit
    - Best place for traditional items, dry fruits and local products
    - A must visit for tourists

DAY TRIPS FROM GILGIT (1-2 hours away)
----------------------------------------
- Hunza Valley (2 hrs) - Attabad Lake, Baltit Fort, Altit Fort, Eagle's Nest
- Rakaposhi Viewpoint (1.5 hrs) - Stunning views of Rakaposhi 7788m peak
- Fairy Meadows (3-4 hrs) - Base camp views of Nanga Parbat

TRADITIONAL FOOD OF GILGIT
----------------------------
1. Mantu (Manto)
   - Traditional dumplings filled with spiced minced meat
   - Served with yogurt and tomato sauce
   - A must-try local dish

2. Chapshuro (Chap Churu)
   - Traditional flatbread stuffed with spiced minced meat
   - Baked in a traditional stone oven
   - One of the most famous dishes of Gilgit-Baltistan
   - Available in local restaurants and homes

3. Trout Fish
   - Fresh trout fish from the cold mountain rivers and streams
   - Countryside Resort arranges trout fishing for guests
   - Best eaten grilled or fried with local spices
   - A specialty of Gilgit-Baltistan

OTHER LOCAL SPECIALITIES
-------------------------
- Dried apricots and apricot oil - famous throughout Pakistan
- Mulberry products - fresh and dried
- Local honey from mountain bees
- Sheer Chai (pink salty tea) - traditional Gilgiti tea
- Diram Phitti - traditional local bread
